In April, we spent a few unforgettable days in Puerto Escondido, a coastal town in Oaxaca, Mexico. Known for its stunning beaches and vibrant surf culture, Puerto Escondido offers a blend of adventure and relaxation that's hard to find elsewhere.

Getting to Puerto Escondido

Puerto Escondido is accessible by various means:

  • By Plane: The closest airport is Puerto Escondido International Airport (PXM), which is just a short drive from the town center. Direct flights are available from Mexico City and other major Mexican cities.
  • By Bus: There are several bus services from Oaxaca City, taking about 10-12 hours due to the winding mountain roads. It’s a scenic, albeit lengthy, journey.
  • By Car: Renting a car can provide more flexibility for exploring the surrounding areas. The drive from Oaxaca City takes approximately seven hours.

Weather and Best Time to Visit

Puerto Escondido enjoys a tropical climate, with warm temperatures year-round. The best time to visit is during the dry season, from November to April, when the weather is most pleasant and there's minimal rainfall. We visited during the Easter holidays, which is a busy time, but the vibrant atmosphere made it worthwhile.

Our Adventure in Puerto Escondido

On our first day, we explored the beaches near our accommodation. Despite the Easter crowds and strong waves that made swimming less enjoyable, we understood why Puerto Escondido is a surfer's paradise. The waves were impressive, and watching the surfers was exhilarating.
In the evening, I had the unique experience of releasing a baby turtle at a local NGO dedicated to turtle conservation. It was a touching moment, seeing the tiny creature make its way to the sea.
The following day, we sought out a quieter beach for swimming and relaxing. The tranquil setting was perfect for unwinding and enjoying the natural beauty of the area. That evening, we visited Laguna de Manialtepec to witness the magical bioluminescence. Swimming in the lagoon and seeing the plankton glow around us was an awe-inspiring experience that we'll never forget.

Places to Eat in Puerto Escondido

Puerto Escondido offers a variety of dining options to suit all tastes:

  • El Cafecito: A popular spot for breakfast and brunch, known for its delicious pastries and hearty meals.
  • Costenito Cevicheria: Offers fresh seafood with a beautiful ocean view.
  • Almoraduz Cocina de Autor: Perfect for those looking for a gourmet dining experience with a touch of Oaxacan flair.

Value for Money and Budget

Puerto Escondido caters to all budgets. From budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxurious beach resorts, there’s something for everyone. Daily expenses, including meals, activities, and transportation, can be managed comfortably on a budget of 50 to 70 USD per person.

Things to See and Do

In addition to its famous beaches, Puerto Escondido offers a range of activities:

  • Surfing: Zicatela Beach is renowned for its powerful waves and is a must-visit for surf enthusiasts.
  • Turtle Release: Participate in conservation efforts by releasing baby turtles into the ocean.
  • Bioluminescence Tour: Take a night tour to Laguna de Manialtepec and swim amidst the glowing plankton.
  • Visit the Mercado Benito Juárez: Explore this local market for a taste of Oaxacan cuisine and culture.

Conclusion

Puerto Escondido is a gem on the Oaxacan coast, offering a mix of adventure, relaxation, and unique experiences. Whether you’re a surfer, a nature lover, or simply looking to unwind by the sea, Puerto Escondido has something to offer everyone. Pack your bags and get ready to explore the magic of this beautiful destination!
