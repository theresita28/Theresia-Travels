After an exciting time in Cancún, we traveled to Playa del Carmen, a lively town known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and proximity to ancient Mayan sites. Our time in Playa del Carmen was a blend of relaxation, cultural exploration, and unforgettable experiences.

Getting to Playa del Carmen

We traveled from Cancún to Playa del Carmen, a journey of about an hour by car or bus. Playa del Carmen is easily accessible and well-connected by road.

Weather and Best Time to Visit

Playa del Carmen enjoys a tropical climate, making it an ideal destination year-round. The best time to visit is during the dry season, from December to April, when the weather is most favorable for beach activities and exploring the town

Our Playa del Carmen Adventure

Day 1: Arrival

We arrived in Playa del Carmen on Thursday evening and checked into our Airbnb. After settling in, we headed to the nearby beach and spent the evening enjoying the serene atmosphere and stunning views.

Day 2: Tulum and Beach Day

On Friday, we traveled to Tulum to visit the famous ruins. The archaeological site, perched on a cliff overlooking the turquoise sea, was breathtaking. After exploring the ruins, we spent the rest of the day relaxing on Tulum's pristine beach, enjoying the white sand and clear waters.

Day 3: New Year's Eve in Cozumel

We took a day trip to the island of Cozumel on New Year’s Eve. Our snorkeling tour was the highlight of the trip. We explored three different spots, each offering unique underwater experiences. At the first spot, we saw a vibrant coral reef, a turtle, and a ray. The second spot featured crystal-clear water with numerous starfish. The final stop allowed us to stand in the shallow turquoise water and enjoy ceviche while taking in the surroundings.
Returning to Playa del Carmen, we got ready for the New Year's Eve celebrations. We headed to the town center to watch the fireworks on the beach, providing a fantastic backdrop to ring in the New Year.

Places to Eat in Playa del Carmen

Playa del Carmen is known for its diverse culinary scene:

  • La Cueva del Chango: Offers delicious Mexican dishes in a lush, jungle-like setting.
  • Alux Restaurant Bar and Lounge: A unique dining experience inside a natural cave.
  • El Fogón: Famous for its authentic tacos and casual atmosphere

Value for Money and Budget

Playa del Carmen provides a range of accommodations to suit all budgets, from affordable hostels to luxurious resorts. Daily expenses, including meals, activities, and transportation, can be managed on a budget of 50 to 100 USD per person.

Things to See and Do

Playa del Carmen offers a variety of attractions and activities:

  • Visit Tulum Ruins: Explore the ancient Mayan ruins with a stunning ocean backdrop.
  • Snorkeling in Cozumel: Discover the vibrant underwater world and diverse marine life
  • Relax on the Beaches: Enjoy the beautiful beaches of Playa del Carmen and Tulum.
  • Experience the Nightlife: The town offers numerous bars, clubs, and live music venues for a fun night out.

Conclusion

Playa del Carmen is a captivating destination that offers a perfect mix of relaxation, adventure, and cultural experiences. Our time here was filled with beautiful beaches, ancient ruins, and vibrant celebrations, making it a must-visit spot on any travel itinerary.
