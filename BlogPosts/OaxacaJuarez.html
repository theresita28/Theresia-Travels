In April, we embarked on an unforgettable journey to Oaxaca de Juárez, the vibrant capital city of the state of Oaxaca. Known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning archaeological sites, and delectable cuisine, Oaxaca de Juárez offers an immersive experience into the heart of Mexican tradition.

Getting to Oaxaca de Juárez

Oaxaca de Juárez is well-connected and accessible by various means:

  • By Plane: The closest airport is Xoxocotlán International Airport (OAX), located about 12 kilometers south of the city center. Direct flights are available from major Mexican cities like Mexico City, Monterrey, and Guadalajara.
  • By Bus: There are comfortable bus services from Mexico City, Puebla, and other nearby cities. The journey from Mexico City takes approximately six to seven hours.
  • By Car: Driving to Oaxaca de Juárez can be a scenic experience, especially if you're traveling from Puebla or Mexico City. The journey offers stunning views of the countryside and takes around five to six hours.

Weather and Best Time to Visit

Oaxaca de Juárez enjoys a mild climate year-round, with warm days and cool nights. The best time to visit is during the dry season, from November to April, when the weather is most pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Our Oaxaca de Juárez Adventure

On our first day, we explored the heart of Oaxaca de Juárez. We wandered through the Zócalo, the bustling market square, where we soaked in the vibrant atmosphere and browsed through the local markets. The Zócalo is a great place to experience the city's lively culture, with street vendors, musicians, and artisans showcasing their crafts.

The following day, we visited the awe-inspiring archaeological site of Monte Albán. Perched on a hilltop, Monte Albán offers panoramic views of the Oaxaca Valley and a fascinating glimpse into the ancient Zapotec civilization. The site is vast, with impressive pyramids, plazas, and carved stone monuments.

On the third day, our journey took us to Mitla, another significant archaeological site known for its intricate stone mosaics and rich history. From Mitla, we traveled to Hierve el Agua the next day. Hierve el Agua is a natural wonder featuring petrified waterfalls and natural pools. Swimming in these pools with a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains was a highlight of our trip.

On our last day in Oaxaca de Juárez, we indulged in some shopping for souvenirs. The local markets offered a variety of unique items, including handcrafted textiles, pottery, and the famous Oaxacan chocolate, a regional specialty that makes for a delightful gift.

Places to Eat in Oaxaca de Juárez

Oaxaca de Juárez is a food lover's paradise, with numerous dining options that showcase the rich flavors of Oaxacan cuisine:

  • La Biznaga: Offers a modern twist on traditional Oaxacan dishes with a cozy, relaxed atmosphere.
  • Casa Oaxaca: Renowned for its gourmet take on local flavors, featuring beautifully presented dishes.
  • Mercado 20 de Noviembre: A bustling market where you can sample a variety of traditional Oaxacan foods, including tlayudas, mole, and tamales.

Value for Money and Budget

Oaxaca de Juárez offers excellent value for money, with a range of accommodations from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Daily expenses, including meals, transportation, and entrance fees, can be managed comfortably on a budget of 40 to 60 USD per person.

Things to See and Do

In addition to its renowned archaeological sites, Oaxaca de Juárez offers a plethora of activities and attractions:

  • Visit the Santo Domingo Church and Cultural Center: Explore the stunning Baroque architecture and the adjacent museum showcasing regional artifacts.
  • Stroll through the Ethnobotanical Garden: Discover the diverse plant life native to Oaxaca and learn about their traditional uses.
  • Explore the Textile Museum: Delve into the rich textile heritage of Oaxaca, with beautiful displays of traditional weaving techniques and designs.

Conclusion

Oaxaca de Juárez is a treasure trove of cultural and historical wonders, offering a rich and immersive experience for every traveler. From its vibrant markets and ancient ruins to its delectable cuisine and scenic natural pools, Oaxaca de Juárez promises a journey full of discovery and delight. Pack your bags and get ready to explore the magic of this captivating city!
