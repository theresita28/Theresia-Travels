Discovering the Charm of Atlixco

One Sunday in September, I embarked on my first journey to Atlixco, a picturesque town just about three quarters of an hour away from Cholula/Puebla. Known for its beautiful landscapes and rich cultural traditions, Atlixco offers visitors a delightful blend of history, art, and festivities.

Getting to Atlixco

Atlixco is conveniently located and can be easily accessed by various means:

  • By Car: The drive from Cholula or Puebla to Atlixco takes approximately 45 minutes. Renting a car provides flexibility and convenience for exploring the area.
  • By Bus: Regular bus services run from Puebla to Atlixco, making it an affordable and straightforward option. The journey takes about an hour.

Weather and Best Time to Visit

Atlixco enjoys a temperate climate, making it an ideal destination year-round. However, visiting during September and December offers unique experiences with local festivals and seasonal events.

Our Atlixco Adventure

During our visit in September, we wandered around the charming old town center, which was bustling with activity due to a local feria (fair). The vibrant stalls and festive atmosphere provided a wonderful glimpse into the local culture and community.

Atlixco bears a resemblance to Cholula, particularly with its church perched on a hill. Climbing up to the church rewarded us with a stunning view of the surrounding countryside. Along the way, we witnessed the incredible performance of the Voladores de Papantla, an ancient tradition practiced for over a thousand years in honor of the gods of fertility. Watching the voladores descend gracefully from a 30-meter-high pole was a breathtaking experience.

In October, I returned to Atlixco and was greeted by Catrina figures set up in various locations around the town. These iconic skeleton figures symbolize Dia de los Muertos and trace their origins to the painter Jose Guadalupe Posada, who created the ‘Calavera Garbancera’ in 1912. Posada’s figure criticized domestic workers aspiring to the lifestyle of wealthy ladies in post-revolutionary Mexico City, and today, Catrinas are a vibrant part of Mexican culture and festivities.

In December, Atlixco transforms into a winter wonderland with its Villa Iluminada. The streets are adorned with beautiful fairy lights, creating a magical atmosphere. Visitors can take photos with Santa, the Grinch, and various other beloved characters like Batman and Minions, making it a delightful experience for families and children.

Places to Eat in Atlixco

Atlixco offers a variety of dining options where you can savor traditional Mexican cuisine:

  • El Mural de los Poblanos: Known for its authentic Pueblan dishes and beautiful murals.
  • Las Calandrias: Offers a charming atmosphere and a diverse menu, perfect for a relaxing meal after exploring the town.
  • Café de la Cruz: A cozy café ideal for enjoying a coffee and pastries while soaking in the local ambiance.

Value for Money and Budget

Atlixco is an affordable destination with options for all budgets. From budget-friendly inns to more comfortable hotels, accommodations are reasonably priced. Daily expenses, including meals and activities, can be managed comfortably on a budget of 30 to 50 USD per person.

Things to See and Do

Atlixco is rich in cultural and natural attractions:

  • Visit the Old Town Center: Explore the historic buildings, vibrant markets, and enjoy the local fairs.
  • Witness the Voladores de Papantla: Experience this mesmerizing traditional performance
  • Climb to the Church on the Hill: Enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Explore the Catrina Figures: Visit during October to see these iconic figures and learn about their cultural significance.
  • Experience Villa Iluminada: Wander through the beautifully lit streets during December and take photos with festive characters.

Conclusion

Atlixco is a hidden gem that offers a rich tapestry of cultural experiences, historical landmarks, and festive events. Whether you're marveling at the ancient traditions of the Voladores de Papantla, admiring the artistic Catrina figures, or enjoying the magical Villa Iluminada, Atlixco promises an enchanting and memorable visit. Pack your bags and discover the charm and beauty of this delightful town!
