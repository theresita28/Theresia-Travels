An unforgettable visit to Zacatlán

In May this year, we visited the charming city of Zacatlán in the state of Puebla, known for its impressive murals and the famous Reloj Floral. Travelling to Zacatlán was not only an opportunity to experience the breathtaking nature and rich culture, but also an adventure full of new impressions and discoveries.

Travelling to Zacatlán

Zacatlán is relatively easy to reach. The nearest airport is Puebla Airport (Hermanos Serdán International Airport), which is around 130 kilometres away. There are various ways to reach the city from the airport:

  • By car: the journey takes around two hours. It is advisable to hire a car to have the flexibility to explore the surrounding sights comfortably.
  • By public transport: There are regular bus services from Puebla to Zacatlán. The bus journey takes around three hours and offers a cost-effective alternative to travelling by car.

Weather and best time to visit

Zacatlán has a temperate climate with mild temperatures all year round. The best time to visit Zacatlán is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, temperatures are pleasant and rainfall is minimal. During the summer months (June to August) it can rain more frequently, but this bathes the landscape in a lush green colour and makes the natural wonders even more impressive.

Getting around Zacatlán

In Zacatlán itself, it is easy to get around on foot, especially in the city centre. For more distant destinations, such as the Piedras Encimadas nature reserve, it is advisable to hire a car or use a local taxi or transport service.

Places of interest in Zacatlán

Our trip to Zacatlán began with a visit to the Piedras Encimadas Nature Reserve, an area full of fascinating rock formations. This natural wonder offers numerous hiking trails and breathtaking views that will delight any nature lover.
In the evening, we explored the town and enjoyed an enchanting puppet show in the clock museum, an experience that fascinates young and old alike. The following day was dedicated to exploring Zacatlán's cultural treasures. We admired the colourful murals that characterise the cityscape and the magnificent Reloj Floral, a floral clock that is considered the city's landmark. The view of the gorge rounded off our day perfectly.

Culinary experiences in Zacatlán

Zacatlán offers a variety of culinary delights. Some recommended restaurants are:

  • El Turista Café: Famous for its traditional dishes and cosy atmosphere.
  • Restaurante La Terraza: With magnificent views of the surroundings and a selection of regional specialities.
  • El Mirador: A perfect place to enjoy a delicious meal with a breathtaking view.

Value for money and budget

Zacatlán is an affordable destination that offers a great balance between experience and cost. Accommodation ranges from budget guesthouses to comfortable hotels that cater for all budgets. An average daily budget of 40 to 60 USD per person is enough to travel comfortably, eat well and visit the main sights.

Conclusion

Zacatlán is a hidden gem in Mexico that delights with its rich culture, impressive nature and warm hospitality. Whether you are looking for an adventure in nature, discover cultural treasures or simply want to unwind, Zacatlán has something for everyone. Pack your bags and let yourself be enchanted by the magic of this wonderful city!
